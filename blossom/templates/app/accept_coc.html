Code of Conduct

Before continuing, please take a moment to accept our Code of Conduct. You are expected to abide by this Code at all times when acting as a Transcriber.

Follow the Format Guides

All transcriptions must follow our format guides. This helps make all transcriptions consistent and shows a good first impression to other users.
This is mandatory.
Consistently ignoring formatting after warnings from the mods will result in a temporary ban (see below).

Be Aware of Your Surroundings

As a transcriber, you are temporarily the face of /r/TranscribersOfReddit and a guest in another community. Be courteous and friendly. If you do not know the answer to someone’s question, refer them to our FAQ or modmail. Do not be rude or sarcastic, even to trolls: we have suggestions about responding to questions and criticism here.

Use Formal Language

When talking to other users in your capacity as a Transcriber, please always use (relatively) formal language and proper grammar (capitalization, no abbreviations, and punctuation as appropriate).

Ask Us if You Need Help

If you have any questions or find yourself in a situation that you cannot properly resolve, you can reach us through modmail the Discord server, or by mentioning a mod by username.

Check the Parent Sub’s Rules

Some partner subreddits have rules against posts with personal information, such as usernames. These posts should not be transcribed, even if they appear in the queue. Transcribing a rule-breaking post can result in bans from the parent subreddit as well as penalties from /r/TranscribersOfReddit.

Penalties

Failure to follow the above rules after a warning from a moderator is worthy of a temporary ban of 7 days. This is to allow time for further discussion with the mods about how to be a good transcriber. If issues continue, a permanent ban may be issued. We are providing a service for those who need it, and these rules help us do that to the best of our ability. Failing to follow them means our service is no longer helpful.
